The three Bandie modes

Bandie is built around what you are trying to do.

Most music tools only serve one group. Bandie connects individual musicians, band workspaces and event organisers so discovery, preparation and promotion all meet in one place.

🎸

For Players

Create a musician profile that shows what you play, what you like, where you are based and when you are available.

  • Promote yourself to bands and organisers.
  • Find bands looking for members or deps.
  • Find local gigs and playing opportunities.
How it works
🥁

For Bands

Create a public band profile and a private workspace for everything the band needs to rehearse, organise and perform.

  • Promote the band to organisers and players.
  • Manage line-ups, songs, setlists and readiness.
  • Coordinate rehearsals, gigs and availability.
How it works
🎟️

For Organisers

Search for suitable bands, check availability, organise gigs and generate the content needed to promote the event.

  • Find bands by genre, location, price and rating.
  • Plan dates and enquiries in one place.
  • Create gig pages, posters and social content.
How it works
For Players

Get seen. Find people to play with.

Bandie gives individual musicians a profile that helps them be discovered by bands, other players and event organisers. It is for guitarists, singers, drummers, bassists, keys players, deps and anyone looking for the next musical opportunity.

For Bands

Look professional outside. Stay organised inside.

Bandie gives every band a public promotion page and a private operating workspace. It is built for amateur bands without managers who still need to organise songs, setlists, members, rehearsals, availability and gigs.

How it works for bands

Promote the band, coordinate the people, and track whether the band is ready to play.

Band mode
1

Register the band

Create a public band profile with genre, location, lineup, media links, pricing guidance and booking CTA.

2

Manage the line-up

Add members, roles and permissions. Invite players, manage deps and keep member access controlled.

3

Build the working songbook

Store song links, parts, notes, folders, setlists and track song readiness for upcoming gigs.

4

Organise rehearsals and gigs

Poll availability, propose rehearsal series, confirm gig dates and publish public availability when ready.

For Organisers

Find the right band and promote the gig.

Why it works

Bandie connects public discovery with private preparation.

A band can be discovered publicly, then prepare privately. Organisers see the polished profile and public availability. Members see the song folders, setlists, notes and readiness tasks.

Core capabilities

Everything the early platform needs, without becoming heavy.

Bandie should stay lightweight and practical. The goal is less admin, fewer lost messages and a clearer route from discovery to gig readiness.
